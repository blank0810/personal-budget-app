title: Payment Links on Invoices
status: previous
description: >
  Get paid faster. You can now attach a payment link to any invoice while you
  draft it, and your client gets a clear way to pay: a "Pay this invoice" button
  and a scannable QR code on both the invoice and its PDF. The link works with
  any provider — a Wise or PayPal link, a GCash link, or a link from any payment
  gateway — so whatever gets you paid, you can put it on the invoice. Leave it
  blank and invoices behave exactly as before.
features:
  - title: Attach a Payment Link While Drafting
    items:
      - "**A new optional Payment Link field** on the invoice form. When creating or editing an invoice, paste any link you use to get paid and it travels with that invoice."
      - "**Provider-agnostic by design.** It isn't tied to any single service — a Wise or PayPal link, a GCash link, or a checkout link from any payment gateway all work the same way."
      - "**Per-invoice, not a global setting.** Each invoice carries its own link, so you can use a different one per client or per job — handy when you generate a link with the exact amount already filled in."
      - "Leave it empty and nothing changes: the invoice renders exactly as it did before."
  - title: One Click or One Scan for Your Client
    items:
      - "When a payment link is present, the invoice and its PDF show a **Pay this invoice** button alongside an **auto-generated QR code**."
      - "Your client clicks the button on screen or scans the QR with their phone — no copying a URL out of a PDF."
      - "The pay prompt shows on sent and overdue invoices and is hidden on the PAID receipt, so a paid invoice never nags for payment."
      - "Links are validated to be secure (https) before they're saved, so what reaches your client is safe to click."
